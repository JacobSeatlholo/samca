About SAMCA

Our Mandate

Reimagining mining as a force where measurable impact replaces symbolic commitment.

SAMCA exists to solve one of the mining industry's most persistent challenges: the disconnect between mining operations and the communities that host them.

Progress is reflected in both economic performance and community wellbeing. We provide the frameworks, advisory services, and platforms that enable mining companies to secure their social license to operate, unlock long-term value, and attract global capital through evidence-based impact.

The Summit introduces a complementary and solution-focused platform that prioritises integration, inclusion, and shared economic value within mining communities.

What We Provide

Our Services

Strategic Advisory Services

Expert guidance to mining houses navigating ESG performance and community engagement frameworks.

Community Engagement Frameworks

Structured development frameworks that prioritise community representation, trust, and partnership.

Investor-Aligned Sustainability Models

Enabling long-term value through evidence-based impact strategies that attract global capital.

Framework

Our Summit Pillars

Why Attend?

Africa's most resource-rich sector, under-integrated in local participation.

Gain first-hand knowledge on North West economic development and national mining trends. The Mining Integration Summit addresses the gap by creating a platform where Policy Meets Implementation → Capital Meets Opportunity → Innovation Meets Industry Demand.

01

Pillar One

Identifying Needs, Gaps & Opportunities

The Summit will provide mining houses with a structured platform to identify operational challenges and strategic opportunities.

  • Operational challenges affecting productivity and social licence
  • Supply chain gaps within the mining ecosystem
  • Strategies to improve ESG performance and community engagement
02

Pillar Two

Needs, Participation & Shared Prosperity

Creating space for community representatives to highlight their development priorities and economic aspirations.

  • Local economic development needs
  • Opportunities for community-owned enterprises
  • Skills development priorities
  • Mechanisms to strengthen trust between communities and mining houses
03

Pillar Three

Empowerment & Leadership

The Forum is focused on increasing representation and creating pathways to leadership for underrepresented groups.

  • Increasing women's participation across the mining value chain
  • Expanding procurement opportunities for women-owned enterprises
  • Addressing barriers to entry within mining careers
04

Pillar Four

Pathways to Opportunity

The Mining Integration Summit will introduce a Youth in Mining Platform focused on building the next generation of sector leaders.

  • Connecting youth with training and apprenticeship opportunities
  • Showcasing youth-led innovation in mining technologies
  • Facilitating investment and mentorship for young entrepreneurs
  • Building pathways from education into the mining workforce
05

Pillar Five

Compliance & Policy Through Integration

Key indicators that highlight the strategic importance of the mining industry to the South African economy.

  • Mining contributes approximately 7–8% of South Africa's GDP
  • The sector supports more than 450,000 direct jobs
  • Mining exports generate over R1 trillion annually
06

Pillar Six

School Innovation Program

The programme invites schools to participate in a structured competition where learners develop practical, innovative, community-focused solutions addressing mining challenges.

Mining Integration Summit 2026

About the Summit

The Mining Integration Summit 2026 marks the first platform of its kind.

Dedicated to strengthening the relationship between mining houses, host communities, investors, and government. Across Africa, mining remains one of the most powerful drivers of economic growth, yet the long-term success of the sector depends on integration, accountability and shared benefit.
